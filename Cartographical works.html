Cartography




Maps serve multiple functions. In their purest form, they should depict specific areas and enhance orientation in the terrain. Additionally, maps serve as a foundation for project planning or visualizations. They can also be appreciated as works of art. A well-prepared map reads itself; a bad one complicates life.
Please, take a sample of my cartographical work and do not hesitate to contact me.



The provided illustration depicts
an overview of the Educational Path, which full form can be accesed upon clicking the image. To enhance the 3D view of the hilly terrain in southern Poland, I generated a terrain hillside, serving as a greyscale background.
I utilized freely available 1m x 1m DEM data tiles downloaded from the Polish National Geoportal.







The provided image represents
a snapshot from the Forest Road Network Project, a requirement for completing one of the courses during my Master’s Degree program. As per a decree issued
by the General Director of State Forests, each Forestry District
(basic organizational unit of National Forests) must establish such a network. This solution rigorously governs the use
of public and private roads for activities like wood transportation and addresses safety concerns.
Furthermore, the existence of such network could serve as protection against potential legal issues.
