Spatial analyses

Spatial analysis is a process of interpreting, exploring, and modelling geographic data to extract meaningful insights and patterns. It involves the use of techniques to analyse
and visualize spatial data, enabling users to identify relationships, trends, and correlations between objects, events, and places. The importance of spatial analysis lies in its ability
to provide actionable insights that inform decision-making in various fields, such as agriculture, forestry, marine sciences, oil and gas, mining, demography, and economy.
By leveraging spatial analysis, organizations and individuals can optimize resource allocation, mitigate risks, and improve outcomes in their respective domains.



Ventilation corridors consist of a network of interconnected pathways where city ventilation occurs, enhancing overall urban climate conditions, such
as reducing maximum temperatures within the city. The map displays selected roughness indices that I computed for the Mokotów district of Warsaw city, aiming
to integrate scientific literature on this subject. These parameters are extensively utilized by numerous urbanists and city planners in various publications.




Suitability analysis stands as a cornerstone in spatial analysis methodology. This technique aims to identify the optimal area or areas that meet specific criteria. Typically conducted on raster data, the analysis assigns each pixel to a distinct class or probability rate, with higher values indicating greater suitability. The image illustrates the outcome of a suitability analysis conducted for my Bachelor’s Thesis. Further details can be found here.




Another form of spatial analysis is known as viewshed analysis, which allows for determining the visible range for input objects relative to their surroundings, utilizing elevation data. Viewshed analysis can be utilized, for example, to assess the potential impact of adding large buildings or to ascertain the actual visibility range of items like radio transmitters or tourist viewpoints.
